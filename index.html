PyCode Conference 2021

PyCode Conference is a conference devoted to the Python language.
It is the meeting place for both experts and beginners.

The aim of the conference is to raise qualifications of participants and highlight the latest trends in Python. It is also important for us to spread good programming practices.

The conference is addressed to all Python programmers. It opens up to the community at every level of experience which means it is intended for advanced specialists as well as for beginners, including students.

Dr. Heloise Stevance - Open source: A scientist's perspective

Keynote speaker

Originally born and raised in France, moved to the UK to study Physics and Astronomy at the University of Sheffield. After working as a support astronomer at the Isaac Newton Group in La Palma for a year, she obtained her Masters of Physics in 2015. She subsequently started a PhD studying the 3D shape of Core Collapse Supernovae, and earned her title in Spring 2019. She joined the University of Auckland as a Research Fellow to research the evolution of massive stars to better understand how they die and produce Supernovae and Kilonovae.

Started public outreach work during her doctorate studies, in early 2016, and have not stopped since.

Open source: A scientist's perspective

One of the great powers of Python, and one of the reasons it has become the most used language in astronomy, is that it is open source. A large ecosystem of mathematical and data analysis packages already exist, but some science needs simply cannot be filled by big packages with large audiences. But just because your application is niche does not mean you should shy away from sharing it with the world - especially in a job where reproducibility is everything. As a computational astrophysicisit who uses and develops some of the nichest python packages, I will present my perspective on the fundamental role that open source has to play in making better science.
