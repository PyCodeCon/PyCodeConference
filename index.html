PyCode Conference 2021

PyCode Conference is a conference devoted to the Python language.
It is the meeting place for both experts and beginners.

The aim of the conference is to raise qualifications of participants and highlight the latest trends in Python. It is also important for us to spread good programming practices.

The conference is addressed to all Python programmers. It opens up to the community at every level of experience which means it is intended for advanced specialists as well as for beginners, including students.

Dr. Heloise Stevance - Open source: A scientist's perspective

Keynote speaker

Originally born and raised in France, moved to the UK to study Physics and Astronomy at the University of Sheffield. After working as a support astronomer at the Isaac Newton Group in La Palma for a year, she obtained her Masters of Physics in 2015. She subsequently started a PhD studying the 3D shape of Core Collapse Supernovae, and earned her title in Spring 2019. She joined the University of Auckland as a Research Fellow to research the evolution of massive stars to better understand how they die and produce Supernovae and Kilonovae.

Started public outreach work during her doctorate studies, in early 2016, and have not stopped since.

Open source: A scientist's perspective 40 mins keynote talk

One of the great powers of Python, and one of the reasons it has become the most used language in astronomy, is that it is open source. A large ecosystem of mathematical and data analysis packages already exist, but some science needs simply cannot be filled by big packages with large audiences. But just because your application is niche does not mean you should shy away from sharing it with the world - especially in a job where reproducibility is everything. As a computational astrophysicisit who uses and develops some of the nichest python packages, I will present my perspective on the fundamental role that open source has to play in making better science.

This talk will discuss offensive and defensive parts of cybersecurity and how Python is helping both sides of the coin, i.e. attackers to make high-level, sophisticated attacks and defenders to develop more robust and flexible solutions. Automating malware process scanning with Python3 60 mins talk

Wanna see how we can make life easier by automating malware and threat analysis using python? Python Pivots

Python is the most widely used programming language both in natural sciences and in data science. This supposedly shared skill has led to a whole new set of career paths for scientists seeking to venture out of fundamental research, but the switch from "science-science" (warning: irony) to a computer-science-dominated field is not without pitfalls.

Through the lens of my own Python Pivot from astrophysics to developing Heat, I'll attempt to reconcile two quite different mindsets, reflect on under-the-radar hurdles, and hopefully convince you that "cutting yourself some slack" is as fundamental a skill as "problem-solving" or "analytical thinking".

Random Number Generator using a Variation Quantum Circuit 15 mins talk

We will be building a random number generator using variational quantum circuit. While a very basic random number generator can be created using a simple quantum circuit, it turns out it has some serious limitations, like the very limited number of unique random numbers generated, and the qubits requirement. So we propose a variational quantum RNG, where we have tried to use a varying parameter to generate a list of unique random numbers. We found that with the variational RNG, we were able to generate the required list of unique random numbers, and with a lower qubit requirement, executing the circuit with only two qubits.

This talk will be based on a team project: Jeeta Jaishikhaa Ghosh (Speaker), Adrija Goswami, Harshdeep Singh.

Introduction to Quantum Computation using Qiskit 45 mins tutorial

Quantum Computation is all the hype right now in the research world, with its applications in various fields like data science, machine learning, finance, chemistry, optimisation and many more. But before we can dive into the advanced and complex topics in quantum computation, we need to understand the basics. In this tutorial, I will present the basics of quantum computation, starting from the description of qubits, and how quantum computation differs from the classical computation by considering the example of a coin toss, a 'classical' coin and a 'quantum' coin. I would then discuss some other concepts from quantum mechanics that form the basis of the quantum computation in general, while using the Qiskit package in Python side by side to give this tutorial a more hands-on outlook. I will use Qiskit to demonstrate all these concepts like the coin toss, quantum states superposition, the application of quantum gates on qubits, ending with a brief introduction to quantum entaglement; teleportation, which for many of us still seems as a fancy concept used in sci-fi movies; and superdense coding.
